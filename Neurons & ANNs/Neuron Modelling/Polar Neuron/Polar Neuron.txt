Polar Neuron

A new neural model made of competing polar subneuron modules within a single neuron using opposing activation patterns.

The Polar Neuron is a model of competing polar subneuron modules within a single neuron using opposing activation patterns.

The core concept behind the Polar Neuron lies in its ability to simultaneously process and integrate information from multiple sources through independent yet interconnected subneurons. Each of these polar subneurons is designed as an autonomous unit capable of performing localized computations, similar to biological neurons. However, instead of relying on a single output signal, each subneuron generates two distinct activation patterns: one representing the "positive" or excitatory state and another for the "negative" or inhibitory state. These opposing activations are then compared within the neuron's decision-making mechanism, resulting in an overall balanced response that integrates both positive and negative influences.

Polar Neuron incorporates a nested architecture where each polar subneuron is itself composed of multiple competing units. This hierarchical structure allows for more granular control over information processing at different levels within the network. The top-level decision mechanism compares the outputs from these lower-level subneurons to determine the final activation state of the entire neuron, effectively weighing and integrating both positive and negative influences across all available channels.
